Our Story

The Club Started as a Conversation.

For more than twenty years, Shankar and Ryan have shared careers, marriages, kids, and a standing habit of sneaking out for the occasional midday round.

East | West wasn’t born from a business plan. It grew from one question they kept coming back to:

Why doesn’t Seattle have a place we’d actually want to spend the rest of the afternoon?

The Idea

The Best Part Came After.

Some rounds were great. Some were forgettable.

What neither of them forgot was what happened afterward. Sitting a little longer. Talking about work, family, ideas, or nothing at all. The kind of afternoons that weren’t planned but somehow became the ones you remembered.

Eventually, they realized they weren’t trying to find a better place to golf.

They were trying to find a better place to spend the rest of the day.

The Idea

Why East | West?

Growing up, Shankar watched people come and go through his family’s front door without needing an invitation. There was always food on the table, conversation somewhere in the house, and one more person staying for dinner than anyone expected.

Years later, visits to India reminded him of that same feeling. Clubs weren’t just places to play golf. They were where people worked for an hour, had lunch with family, caught up with friends, celebrated milestones, or simply stopped in because they had nowhere else they’d rather be.

Ryan had been looking for that kind of place too.

East | West is their attempt to bring that rhythm of everyday life to Seattle.

“We weren’t trying to build another country club. We were trying to build somewhere we’d stop by on an ordinary Tuesday.”
Shankar & Ryan
